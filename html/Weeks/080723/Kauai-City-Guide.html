Kauai City Guide

Kauai delivers nature in a way unmatched by any other place on earth. There is a reason movies like "Jurassic Park," "Blue Hawaii," and "Raiders of the Lost Ark" shot here. In a word: splendorous. From the plunging depths of Waimea Canyon, to the towering, jagged cliffs of the Napali coast, to the tropical fronds, delicate orchids and misty-shrouded vines of lavish greenery, the Island tantalizes. Kauai wears the tropical crown of Hawaii and is know as the Garden Isle.

You don't even have to step off the plane to notice Kauai is different. As you descend into that canopy of shimmering green, teeming with life and dotted with more waterfalls, beaches, and bays per square mile than any of her Island sisters, Kauai sends your senses into hyper drive. Bursts of opulent color, singsong calls of tropical birds, serenades of traditional musicians, ocean spouts shooting water in the air amid a backdrop of cliffs, canyons, waterfalls, and golden beaches. Kauai is alive with adventure and romance.

Kauai may be the Hawaiian Island with the longest history, but it's still a sleepy little island and the laid-back home to about 60,000 residents. Back during the time of Captain James Cook and western discovery, however, Kauai had her challenges. King Kamehameha was working hard to bring all of the Hawaiian Island under his unified rule, but Kauai and its smaller neighbor Niihau resisted as Kamehameha's armada of ships and canoes tried to take the islands by force, twice. (Thanks to a storm and then an epidemic; seems nature has been on Kauai's side from the beginning.) Eventually Kauai's reining king peacefully joined the Hawaiian kingdom without the shedding of blood.

Kauai draws a diverse audience thanks to its dramatic splendor, its proliferation of things to do, and true island atmosphere. And when there's nature like this-you can bet a mind-staggering array of nature-loving activities aren't far behind. Check out the 3,000-foot cliffs of the Napali coast by helicopter. Hike the Waimea Canyon for unforgettable views of the "Grand Canyon of the Pacific." Kayak in the only navigable rivers of the Hawaiian chain. Swim. Snorkel. Sunbathe. Golf. Visit the Spouting Horn and watch the ocean defy gravity as it shoots 50 feet up in the air. And don't be surprised if you find yourself giving way to the mystic charm of it all. Because one thing's for sure-the Garden Isle has a magic all its own.
